give children in poverty TUTORING AND COMPUTER PROGRAMS

Many children in poverty leave school without a basic education – if they’re able to attend at all. Globally, 260 million children aren’t even in school.

When children and youth fall behind, usually due to illness or family problems, the resources to catch up don’t exist. Overcrowded classrooms and uneducated parents can make it nearly impossible for children to get the foundational knowledge they need to keep growing and thriving. This makes getting a stable job or planning for the future seem hopeless.

“A changed child!”

Sangita now receives straight A's

On days like today, when a chronic illness weakens her mother, Sangita must stay home to help with chores and look after her younger sister. The 11-year-old’s school attendance suffers. When she does make it to class, Sangita struggles with schoolwork.

More “A changed child!” Read less

Her parents are part of the approximately 25% of India’s population who are illiterate. Sangita’s mom and dad know only how to sign their names. Sangita, a first-generation learner, cannot rely on them for help with homework.

Thanks to tutoring sessions in Kolkata, India, Sangita is getting the extra help she needs to improve her grades. The tutoring program there focuses on providing support in English, language arts, math and science.

“In group tutoring, the teachers take so much care to make sure I understand everything,” Sangita says. “I’ve made five or six friends who help me. They encourage me to ask the teacher whenever I have a problem.”

Although Sangita must still miss school on the days her mother is sick, her grades remain high. She continues to score A’s and even A-pluses after just one year of attending the group tutoring classes.

Daimanti, Sangita’s mother, has noticed a significant change in her daughter. “Previously, Sangita couldn’t be woken up early in the morning to go to school but now she wakes up on her own. Even in the evening I no longer have to remind her to study,” she says. “Sangita is a changed child!”

All ofVerdant Charity ’s 13 field agencies offer tutoring in math and literacy. This support helps kids learn basic skills, pass their current grade and feel more confident about finding a job in the future.

“A daily spark”

Computer skills give Anita the drive to succeed

Intelligent. Quick learner. Vibrant. Role model. These are all words used to describe Anita, whose cheerful smile brightens the room as she enters her computer class at her community center in Guatemala.

Anita is always ready to work, with a positive attitude and kind words for everyone around her. Anita’s optimism is even more remarkable considering that growing up in a small, impoverished village is the least of her challenges.

More "A daily spark" Read less

Anita was born without legs—and has spent her 19 years navigating an environment that can be discriminatory and cruel toward the less able-bodied. Yet Anita perseveres, choosing to face life with a smile and a “can do” attitude that astonishes those around her. “It’s important to make the most of every minute of our lives, to not waste it,” she says.

Anita’s favorite class is the computer course, because the knowledge she gains will help her achieve her goal of a degree in accounting with an emphasis on computer science.

One of her favorite memories was winning a medal as the best student in the course. “That was the biggest source of inspiration in my life because I had never won first place. I’d received honors, but not a medal!”

Her computer instructor becomes emotional as he talks about her. “As a student and human being, she is the best I’ve known. She inspires me to do my best.”

Anita’s familiarity with technology will help her find a job that allows her to use her greatest asset—her bright, inquisitive mind. “I want to show the world that, despite my disabilities, I can be someone,” she says confidently. “The help I receive is like a daily spark.  ”

Each course is held in one ofVerdant Charity ’s 67+ community centers. The centers are a safe haven where many kids have their only real access to digital technology.

Who we are

  • We're a top-rated, international charity organization and a community of passionate world-changers.
  • With your help, we're working to end global poverty once and for all.
  • We believe every kid deserves a chance.
  • We're focused on impact — we work in four key areas to help kids break the cycle of poverty: health, education, empowerment and employment.

Looking for more? Learn aboutVerdant Charity .

Your support helps us focus on 4 key areas

  • Health : to help kids develop healthy habits and connect them to services.
  • Education : to help children complete secondary school.
  • Empowerment : so kids can build leadership, teamwork and confidence.
  • Employment : to help teens develop workplace and technical skills to change their life for good.
  • Kids gain access to program activities in community centers — 70+ around the world.

How you help

  • With your help, kids receive life-changing benefits like medical care, educational support, life skills and job placement after graduation.
  • Your gift also supports  70+ community centers world-wide which offer safe spaces for kids to learn and grow.
  • Your support creates a ripple effect for generations.
  • Your gift changes lives — for good.
